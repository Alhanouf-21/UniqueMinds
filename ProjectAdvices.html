Advices For Parents

* Seek Support and Information *

Connect with support groups, online forums, and community 
organizations that cater to parents of children with intellectual disabilities.
These networks can provide valuable advice, resources, and
emotional support.

* Educate Yourself *

Take the time to learn about your child's specific disability, including its 
characteristics, challenges,and available resources. Understanding your 
child's needs can help you advocate effectively and make informed decisions.

*Focus on Abilities, Not Limitations*

Celebrate your child's strengths, talents, and achievements. Focus on what 
your child can do rather than dwelling on limitations. Encourage their 
interests and passions

* Set Realistic Expectations *

Set achievable goals for your child based on their abilities and developmental
stage, Celebrate progress no matter how small, and avoid comparing 
your child to others
